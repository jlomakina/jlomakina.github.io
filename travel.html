My Travel Destinations

“Travel is fatal to prejudice, bigotry, and narrow-mindedness, and many of our people need it sorely on these accounts. Broad, wholesome, charitable views of men and things cannot be acquired by vegetating in one little corner of the earth all one's lifetime.” -Mark Twain

When I travel, I learn more about culture, people, and differences than I ever could from a textbook. Nothing can replace seeing different parts of the world in person. You cannot truly understand how others live until you live with them. I am so grateful to have had the opportunity to visit so many countries and I thank my parents for inspiring this passion for me. All over the world, people show kindness and selflessness, regardless of race, religion, or culture. I would like to share some of my stories with you.

Morocco
I visited Morocco with my mom and her fiance in late 2016. One of the days, we set to wander the narrow streets of Fez in search of the famous leather tannery hidden somewhere in the depths of this 450-acre medina. With no signs to follow, we began asking locals on how to find the needle in the haystack. This one man, about my age, named Maroul, offered to show us. Despite being repeatedy warned to not follow strangers and that tourists will get taken advantage of, we were so far lost we needed any kind of help we could get. Maroul went to school as to learn how to carve plaster and now sold shoes on the side. After showing us the tanneries, he insisted on showing us the rest of the city. He took us to the local mosques, schools, authentic argan oil shops, carpet shops, a local resturant, and spent over seven hours with us. He told me if I ever had more time he would invite us to dinner with his family, and that he will forever consider us his family. We tried to offer him some money for helping us so much but he refused. I have made a friend for life in Fez. This is why I travel.

Nicaragua
With my two girlfriends during our spring break, we ventured to find a volcanic island called Ometepe. After getting off of a four hour "chicken" bus ride, we began bargaining with the local taxis to take us to the port. A nice local woman could see we were getting ripped off and insisted that we get in her taxi. We were so squished she had to sit on our lap. We laughed during the whole bumpy ride. She sacrificed her comfort to help some strangers save a few dollars. This is why I travel.

Now, by this point you might be thinking that I am spoiled. On the contrary, I am able to travel because this is what I prioritize. Instead of buying new clothes or living in a nice apartment, I choose to put money aside for this. I cannot describe the feeling when I visit somewhere new. It is almost like a child seeing the world for the first time. I could continue forever, but for now, I'll leave you with a quote from Gustave Flaubert, “Travel makes one modest. You see what a tiny place you occupy in the world.”
